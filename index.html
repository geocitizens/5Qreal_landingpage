Keep it simple! Ask 5 smart questions!

Knowledge, Attitudes and Skills (KAS). Knowledge is the first step to adopt a new agricultural innovation. Attitudes towards the "new" depend on a combination of the individualâ€™s belief that it will lead to desired outcome (outcome belief) and the values he or she attributes to those outcomes (social norm). It is identified as peoples perception on a new practice, technology or service. To successfully implement knowledge transfer strategies in agricultural development projects it is important to understand cognitive barriers and drivers for adoption. Skill for subsumption knowledge transfer into farmer practice is the last and main desirable change and it generally occurs because of previous changes in knowledge, skills and/or attitude toward a practice or service.

The 5Q Approach

QT

Smart
Question Trees
__

Simple sets of five questions which querry changes in Knowledge, Attitudes and Skills (KAS) form the basis of the 5Q approach. A logic-question-tree structure is used to keep it simple and smart.

ICT

Internet
Communication Tools
__

ICT channels facilitate more cost-effective data collection. Feedback about project success and KAS of beneficiaries is crowd-sourced at low cost.

DA

Data
Science
__

Iterative, descriptive analytics from raw data and customizable reports are used to enable fast sharing of insights.

DM

Decision
Making
__

Key to institutionalizing feedback systems is making the results publicly available in real time. Therefore, quick and easy visualization of changes is used to provide findings for decision making.
